The TL;DR Guide to Course Expectations

Here is what I propose you concern yourself with from this moment on.

Some goals and some policies

  1. Learn strategies for developing more complex projects
  2. Learn to articulate design scenarios and communicate them
  3. Acquire efficient and low-stress work habits
  4. Finish a concept and have a deliverable by the end of the semester.
  5. Be organized
  6. Do not use a desktop publishing program for anything in this class—I mean anything.
  7. Do not send code via email (that includes .zip attachments). You can: send me links or examples in a sharing application, such as JS Bin, codepen or JSFiddle, or a GitHub Gist. If there is simply an error you want me to look at, send me a link to page in question on your server.
  8. Always author a README for everything you plan on receiving a grade for.

Allow me to go on a brief email rant. Part A—there is a very simple rule to abide by: If you are going to ask a question, the email asking it and the information you provide should be longer than the response you expect. A one line email like I'm having trouble with ___ (something super complicated). Help? is perhaps the most terrifying thing one can imagine in an inbox. If you have a problem with something, write an email that at the very least shows how hard you tried (that means examples and links n stuff). Part B—if you are just letting me know about something (like missing class), it is improbable that I will respond, so no need to email again asking if I got the message.

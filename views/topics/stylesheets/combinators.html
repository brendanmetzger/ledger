css combinators

Descendant selectors are meaningful if not a bit generic; css combinators be employed that allows more careful sifting through documents thus limit the reach of a descendant chain.

Specifying the nature of a relationship has many benefits, starting with the fact that one can document their intention. If one is only looking at a stylesheet and sees something like .intro ~ p { text-indent: 1em; } they can begin to enderstand the nature of a designer’s intention without cross-referencing multiple documents, stylesheets, or worst a foreign api. That sample rule reads to someone, any paragraph following the introduction should be indented.

Ultimately, combinators are not something you use in high concentrations, but they can let you do clever things and save you a heap of time by modifying the nature of a descendant relationship on a simple selector chain.

  1. API here refers to a third party tool or framework that a designer or developer does not author, like bootstrap. In terms of CSS often these tools require an adoption of philosophical approaches that may or may not lead to better concepts or design. When it comes to a rigid and sometimes odd philosophy, I think you should understand all the variables before you take a sip of kool-aid™.
  2. Siblings are any elements that have the same parent. Siblings are adjacent depending on their relationship to one another. Given three children, the middle child has two adjacent siblings, the first and last children are not adjacent to one another.

Regular Expressions

In theoretical computer science and formal language theory, a regular expression is a sequence of characters that define a search pattern, mainly for use in pattern matching with strings, or string matching, i.e. find and replace-like operations. The concept arose in the 1950s, when the American mathematician Stephen Kleene formalized the description of a regular language, and came into common use with the Unix text processing utilities ed, an editor, and grep, a filter.

While regular expressions are truly mind-boggling at times, if you plan on making your living by typing abstract symbols into a computer, then you might as well introduce yourself sooner than later. They are a component of many programming languages, and they syntax and conventions are very consistent. Truly understanding what regular expressions may take years, but you will earn those years back with the amount of time they eventually save you.

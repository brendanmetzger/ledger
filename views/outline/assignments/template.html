※ Keep in Mind

For your homework to be reviewed, you must do two things:

  1. Indicate the time you spent on the project, in hours by adding the following meta Element to the head of your index.html page; <meta name="hours" content="0" /> and replacing the content value from 0 with the hours spent. *
  2. Ask at least one question regarding an obstacle or curiosity you encountered while working by adding a note in the readme.txt file. (you can as much and whatever else you like for that matter). Do not leave the readme.txt file unedited.

* I recommend 5+ hours to gain an understanding of the material. Often it is difficult for me to determine if poor quality work comes from struggle or procrastination. Please do not exaggerate this number to impress me—I would consider it a breach of trust. The reason for this is as follows: I could structure this class like math or any other technical medium; ie., incorrect is incorrect, do problems 1-17. I have decided to be much more liberal because as this is a technical medium I don’t want the mistakes (which I expect) to be punitive, which I feel discourages learning. There are never explicitly right or wrong answers or a specific agenda, nor do I grade your work in a mechanical fashion. So when you say I worked x hours imagine it as nothing more than putting yourself in line; those who spent the most time on any given project will be at the front and get graded when I have the most energy. I think this is the fairest thing to do, but I am always open to suggestion.

Artistic studies are essentially sketches done in preparation for a finished work. In this course, we use studies to show me you are considering the principles of the course without the burden of an expectation for a finished product or expected outcome. Incorporate the learning into your semester-long project. Use this work to add new stuff, try ideas and constantly move forward.

Practice and self directed study are going to be where you gain the vast majority of skills in life. Inability to practice the course material will have a negative effect on your creativity and talent stay persistant. I hope everyone will find the material challenging at some point in this clase, so embrace the challenge and in time you will start to enjoy it. Conversely, if you don’t find the material challenging, read this article about the problem with confidence.

  • I will kid around with this throughout the semester; familiarize yourself with the concept of RTFM

  • Avoid emailing late the night before something due. I don’t want you be stressed out, please don’t stress me out.
  • Not getting an assignment—I understand that. If you don’t know what I’m asking and it is keeping you from getting started, go your own direction and show you are practicing so you don’t fall behind—I can be flexible as long as the effort is there.

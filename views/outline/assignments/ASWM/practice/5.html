An Easy Challenge

Your understanding of JavaScript is increasing, it will be beneficial to practice conventions and refactoring. The trick here is that you will be writing a program and next week someone else will take your program and try to refactor it.

The outline pointed you to a resource that talks about how JavaScript is typically written.

  • do not comment your code
  • do not write notes explaining how it works.
  • do read through the article on naming conventions
  • do use XHR/AJAX at least once to load some kind of content.
  • Really make a concerted effort to write a script around 100 lines tops, 50 lines minimum.
  • Code need not be profound or even interesting, but next week's assignment depends on this one; let that be a motivating force.

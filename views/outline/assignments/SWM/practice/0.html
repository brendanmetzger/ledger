Understanding the Medium of Web Development

The internet—and approaching ubiquity—is the vehicle through which we communicate. We have evolved our writing systems into various broadcast systems and the internet is now the backbone for essentually all distributions of content: broadcast media, written news, and even water-cooler blather (I'm looking at you, twitter). Perhaps there is a discussion to be had on the disadvantages of this ubiquitious system, but for the time being, we'll focus on our role as players in this game.

Most of research studies this semester will allow generic content; lorem ipsum and fpo images—that is, in order to save you some time, I will not require you to provide actual content. However, when I assign topics that do involve content (such as this one), its difficult to find something everyone is interested in. As your instructor, I also struggle getting to know your strengths and weaknesses until they present themselves over the semester. To remedy both problems, the topic is be something I assume everyone is into (at least according to Freud): yourself. I expect you know a thing or two about your own experience, and I'll get to know you a little bit too.

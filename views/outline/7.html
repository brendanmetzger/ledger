Critique

Project Presentations won't be particularly painful—you can treat this as a simple pitch that need not take more than five minutes tops.

For the Presenter

  • Be ready with your work
  • Be ready to say something about your work
  • Highlight any obstacles or difficult parts you were able to solve for
  • Invite constructive criticism
  • Listen: Keep an open mind and avoid being defensive
  • Don’t take it personally
  • Take notes

For the Reviewers

  • What is the presenters project goal? Are they achieving it?
  • Is there any technical advice you are able to give?
  • Separate personal preference from more abstract analysis.
  • Talk as much about what it is, as what it isn’t (people don’t see things the same way)
  • The PNP (positive negative positive) sandwich - surprisingly effective in the real world

Survey & Next Steps

I hope throughout the course of working on a midterm you have identified some weak spots and areas that you need to focus on. When faced with a blank canvas, it becomes incredibly easy to let frustrations get the better of us. I understand this all too well, but hopefully this exercise reinforces that there is a lot of little pieces that fit together in order to make a successful project something that came from your own mind and ingenuity.

In your spare moments over break, find time to revisit material that has piqued your curiosity or left you a little confused. Over the second half of the semester we will be making a transition from research to application.

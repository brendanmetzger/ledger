Preface

Before even considering how to move something with a program, think about it philosophically. Movement is defined as a change between states, like sitting on the left side of the room, we could call state A, and later perhaps, sitting on the right side of the room is state B. There are many ways to get from state A to state B. The easiest, is the instant transition; like the shutter of an eyelid, an object can move from the left side to the right. From there, things get interesting, as we can control many variables of that movement, most notably, time.

The questions from here on out are what can you change and when and how can you change something. With that, here are the practice templates that we will work on today.

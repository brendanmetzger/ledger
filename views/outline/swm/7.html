Positioned Layouts

A typical document is generated into a normal flow, with block-level elements that can be thought of as structural boxes in inline-level elements that flow and wrap like text in a paragraph. I could explain this over and over, but there is so much literature out there, that in the interest of time, I am going to punt to this article from MDN.

In the example below, one thing to take away when implementing a positioned layout is that the elements become layered, like a stack of playing cards. This is important, drawers, light-boxes; essentially anything thit sits on top of anything else must be positioned, because when you position it, you can set the z-index property, giving full control of the stacking order.

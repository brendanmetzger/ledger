End-notes

My father is a student of prose and the act of consciousness, not how to perfectly sharpen cross-cut saws with a triangle file. Yet this did not make him a terrible carpenter, his profession; in fact, it most likely prevented it.

Perspective in any craft matters greatly, just as the carefully studied methods of an apprentice can lead to mastery. Yes, precision matters, as does thought, beauty, and integrity.

As someone who appreciates these things, it is important to me that they do not become antiquities. I think about all the jobs in the world, all the people doing them, and I think about who appreciates what and it is often underwhelming save for scattershot instances of rare effort.

There is nothing so magnificent as the substance of our souls, or a well-honed blade.
